About Me
I'm an undergraduate Computer Engineering student at New York University Abu Dhabi. A field within computer engineering that truly captivates me is health applications—especially those that leverages AI, and microcontrollers. What draws me in is the deeply human aspect of it: knowing how many people, both close to me and complete strangers, live with different conditions, seeking prevention and treatment, doing everything they can just to live a little longer. That reality has driven my passion toward this space—because here, my knowledge can directly impact someone's life.
Beyond the technical world, I absolutely love music. I listen to a lot of indie rock, 90s and early 2000s pop, jazz, city pop, and Sinhala songs that carry waves of nostalgia, some from my own childhood, and others from my parents' days that I can only imagine through sound. I also adore films. The cinephile in me often drawn to movies like The Shawshank Redemption, In the Mood for Love, Good Will Hunting, Fight Club, and anything by Wong Kar-wai, and ones that challenge societal norms and capture the rawness of life. I also enjoy dressing up and styling myself, especially with pieces that carry a vintage or retro vibe. I find joy in curating outfits with unique pieces of clothes/accessories that echo eras gone by.
In my free time, you’ll either find me catching up with friends, picking up skills I’ve been meaning to practice, like playing the guitar or learning tennis, or finally indulging in the media I’ve missed thanks to the chaos of university life.

Key Projects

30 Minutes Film Festival - The Myth Of Productivity

This website critiques the myth of constant productivity in the digital age, especially among university students facing academic pressure and rigid expectations. It explores the emotional toll of this mindset—highlighting exhaustion, disconnection, and the burden of long-term planning—while advocating for balance, spontaneity, and meaningful human connection. Through narrative and video, it contrasts intrusive thoughts with joyful moments, showing how time with loved ones can offer perspective and fulfillment beyond mere achievements.

Interactive Comic

This comic follows a character who briefly encounters a tour guide at the beginning and end of their journey, traveling through various destinations on Earth in between.

Are Dreams Real?

This audio project centers on the anxiety of performance and the pressure to succeed. It captures a heartfelt conversation between a mother and daughter after the daughter wakes up from a dream, revealing her inner worries and emotional vulnerability.

How To Survive A Group Project

This short film captures the challenges of collaborating on a group project—conflicts around time management, varying levels of effort, and the frustration of imbalanced responsibility. It highlights familiar dynamics: the overachiever, the passive participant, and the one full of excuses. Despite the tension and uneven contributions, the project concludes with unfair grading, where even those who did little receive the same credit as those who carried the team.

Machine Learning Projects

Developed a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) from scratch using NumPy to classify handwritten digits from the MNIST dataset. Also implemented a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) without using deep learning libraries to generate synthetic images through adversarial training.
